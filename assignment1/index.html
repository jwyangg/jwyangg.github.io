Endless Summer

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience? The first thing I paid attention to when interacting with the experience was the colour palette, it is very fitting for the theme of summer, especially with the imagery of the palm trees at the beach. I then realized that there was sound playing, it was a very calm and soft sound so I didn’t realize that there was sound at first. The next thing I paid attention to is that nothing happened to the experience, I had to initiate it by resizing the browser, which opened my to the start button and introduced me to the brand “Richard Mille” with the main header “Endless Summer.”

☁️Resizing the browser, making it bigger then smaller to watch the animation of the sun rising and falling
☁️Reading the introductory text (invitation to the experience)
☁️Going through the tutorial
☁️Moving around the screen to view the full picture, due to the size of the browser not containing the full image

☁️Clicking through to “find the secret experience”
☁️Listening and reading to the section where they included a “poetic interlude”

☁️As 2 minutes hit, I finally made it to the next section

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with? There are three sections to the experience: The Archipelago, the Beach, and The City. I spent the most time exploring the first section, The Archipelago, as I was still taking time to learn how the webpage operated and how the creator was intending it to be interacted. I noticed that there were some interactive elements (what they refer to as “secret features”), separate to the interaction where the user can click and hold down to hear the sound of the scene (rather than the sound that was produced from the experience). Because I wasn’t sure of how many interactive elements there were I kept clicking around to see what there was. Furthermore, upon clicking the three dots, an animation with text related to the scene appears. This is in all of the sections, however, I spent a lot of time in the first section engaging with it to not miss a detail. Although, there isn’t a specific element I engaged most with, The Archipelago is definitely the overall aspect I spent most time engaging with.

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience? The most common action in my two minute interaction was clicking and holding on the screen while simultaneously clicking and dragging. I paid more attention to clicking and holding, rather than clicking and dragging as my main objective from clicking and dragging was to continue holding to listen to sounds. So, clicking and dragging was just a side-effect of clicking and holding. As previously explained, when clicking and holding, the user is able to experience the sound of the scene, instead of the music/soundtrack that is provided for the experience. It was the most common action because I wanted to see if the audio was looping, or if there were any hidden elements from listening to the audio. The sounds when clicking and holding include: the beach, the sounds of cars, a person skateboarding, etc. essentially common sounds that can be heard from a Summer holiday.

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interaction with the experience? The opening page introduces the user to the brand “Richard Mille.” I’m already aware that “Richard Mille” is a luxury watch brand, so I was under the assumption that this was to introduce a new watch. As you click “start”, the user is welcomed with the introductory text that explains the website is to tribute the “grand unveiling of the RM 07-01 coloured ceramics.” The main purpose of this interactive experience to promote their releasing watch line. They have achieved this through an immersive, visually aesthetic, experience that mimics a “summer day” and reminisces their upcoming release.

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal of promoting the watch? In order to successfully promote their launch, as it was previously mentioned, an immersive, visually aesthetic experience was created. The final portion of the webpage links to the main “Richard Mille” website to showcase the collection in its entirety. Unlike other promotional materials, this webpage doesn’t start by showcasing the product, rather, they let the user enjoy the “Summer” experience the interactions communicate. After the user experiences all aspects, they are given the option to “Discover the RM 07-01”. In the entire webpage experience, there includes only one photo of the promotional watch at the very end. This visually pleasing experience prompts curiosity of how the aesthetic conveys in the watch, thereby prompting audiences to explore the actual watch being promoted. Therefore, this primary goal is delivered purely after the enjoyment and appreciation of the webpage.

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times) As this webpage was designed to promote a specific line released by “Richard Mille”, it is an experience that is expected to have short-term engagement. Since, this is a promotional website for the “RM 07-01:, it would most likely not be used to update audiences on the status of any other watch lines. Furthermore, the detailed descriptions and other details are on the actual website of “Richard Mille”, instead of this promotional website (Endless Summer).

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time? As this webpage was designed to promote a specific line released by “Richard Mille”, it is an experience that is expected to have short-term engagement. However, based on my personal experience, it may be a website that drives users to visit multiple times to uncover all the “secret features”, as there are multiple layers to explore. This can be assumed by the structure of the experience being divided into three sections, creating the assumption that there are many hidden details to appreciate, which may be easier discovered through multiple visits. The interactive elements, such as clicking and holding or dragging, revealing animations/text, encourages users to take their time to explore each scene, which may create the implication that the website may be better enjoyed in multiple, shorter visits.

What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference? Likewise, that the webpage is designed for promotional purposes for a watch, it takes inspiration from a watch. To navigate through the page, the user must turn the dial located at the bottom of the page. Each of the indentations and the circle dial, imitates that of a watch face, with the time also at the bottom. The dial at the bottom could also be interpreted as the side dial on the watch, especially mimicking the turning function (to navigate the page in this instance). The overall, colour scheme is also very reminiscent of an art/design style called “Memphis Design”. Memphis Design usually refers to architectural elements, typically a combination of shapes and bright colours. Upon looking at the website that is linked at the end of the experience, it can be observed that the watches were in-fact inspired from the Memphis Design style.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience? As it was mentioned previously, the references to a physical watch, guide user behaviour by allowing the user to progress through the environment. If not for the dial, and the movements, the user is unable to progress through and experience the entire page. The dial behaves in a similar way to a clock ticking, the passing of time and also adjusting the side dial to reach the accurate time. Furthermore, if not for the interaction with the dial, because the user is unable to progress through, the final page leading to the website with the actual watch would not be reached, therefore failing to fulfil its role in promoting the watch. These elements depict the importance of thematic presentation. It not only introduces the aesthetics, but also gives insight on what to expect and how to keep everything cohesive.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience? The reference of the watch face/dial communicates the importance of cohesion and thematic aesthetics. The high-quality visuals and design with the refined aesthetics is very fitting to the luxury watch brand and, yet, the website sparks curiosity and engagement on the relevance to the product. Including the discrete reference of the dial, located at the bottom of the page, tied the theme together on how this was a promotional design for a watch.

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating? The most frustrating part of the interaction was actually the speed of navigation and the size of the browser. Because the webpage would only operate when resized into a smaller browser, it felt a bit frustrating that the size was so small. Although a part of the experience was to click and drag the screen to explore the full picture of the webpage, because I personally prefer larger screens, it felt a bit frustrating to look at. Furthermore, I wanted to see the bigger picture to see what the scene looked like overall, to appreciate the visuals, which was not possible in the smaller browser size. In terms of the speed of navigation, sometimes the dial would turn a bit slow, so I would have to overextend my movements to let the experience continue to the next. I also wanted to go back and forth between each section but due to the speed of navigation, it was very difficult, however, although I found the speed frustrating, I feel as though it’s fitting for the theme. This is because it seems to be an experience to incite calmness and relaxation, so it makes sense to work a bit slower.

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying? Surprisingly, I found the most satisfying part of the interaction resizing the browser. It left the strongest impact on me because you would see the sun rising and falling. I personally found it very satisfying because I haven’t seen many things like this. I also found it the most satisfying because the visuals were very pretty, in my personal opinion, and I really like the colour scheme that was used. Although, overall, I found the size of the browser frustrating, I really enjoyed watching the sun rising and falling.
